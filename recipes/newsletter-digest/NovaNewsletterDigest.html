EVERY Katie Parrott · 4 min
The cognitive trap hidden in AI autopilot
AI was supposed to make you smarter. Instead, it may be making you less critical. Parrott identifies processing fluency — the bias where smooth, polished text feels more correct — as the hidden danger in AI-assisted work. AI output always reads confident, triggering the same bias behind 1990s cockpit automation failures.
Takeaway — Write your rough position before reading the AI's answer; force one sentence explaining why you accept the output. Every's “Proof” editor now tracks human-to-machine word ratios as a quality signal.

ENG LEADERSHIP Gregor Ojstersek · 5 min
Weave's AI-only code review pipeline
Weave has eliminated mandatory human code review. Five AI tools — Cursor Bugbot, GrepTile, Cubic, Wispbit, and Continue — run in parallel on every PR, with human review now optional. Paired with a no-planning-beyond-one-month culture and a single 9am standup.
Takeaway — The first concrete, production-scale example of AI-first CI/CD at a startup. A preview of what engineering process looks like when the bottleneck becomes reviewing, not writing.
